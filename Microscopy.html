Microscopy

-by Akash Kumar Singh

In almost every lab I've worked in, my primary focus has been on microscopy, with a particular emphasis on brightfield and fluorescence microscopes. Microscopy, to me, is not just a tool for observation but a remarkable fusion of physics and electronics. The microscope embodies a wealth of scientific principles, from the intricate design of the objective lens and eyepiece to the sophisticated sensor technologies, bridging the fields of optics and semiconductor physics. Through my extensive work with these instruments, I've come to deeply appreciate the stark difference between the theoretical concepts taught in textbooks and their real-world applications. Textbooks often present an idealized and simplified view of optics and semiconductors, which can be far removed from the practical challenges faced in a laboratory setting. In reality, we must navigate and address these idealizations, understanding that the true complexity lies in adapting these concepts to build functional devices that perform effectively in the real world. This recognition of the gap between theory and practice has fueled my ongoing pursuit of knowledge in microscopy. I continually study the underlying concepts to bridge this gap and have discovered several outstanding articles and websites that break down these principles in a clear and fundamental way. For those who wish to deepen their understanding of these topics, I've compiled a list of valuable resources that contains links for topics like Fundamentals of Microscopy, Fluorescence Microscopy, Image Processing used in microscopy which can help one to understand the concepts and use it in their research.
